ARES General Membership Meeting

October 15, 2004

 

On Friday October 15, 2004 a general members meeting was called at the Pembroke Police Services building in Pembroke for 7:30 P.M.. The Friday date was selected to accommodate a visit from the ARES Ottawa DEC Mike Hickey. The meeting was well attended, with the following 19 members present. The meeting agenda included a review of the current status of our 'Needs and Assessment' progress, as well as an update of our existing agreements with surrounding municipalities. A brief was delivered by Fred (VA3FPB) regarding status of OSARVA, and a discussion by Brodie for the upcoming SET 2004. The Halloween patrol and Devils night patrol was discussed, as well as the upcoming Cobden Santa Parade. The floor was turned over to Mike Hickey (VE3IPC) our Ottawa area DEC for an introduction and talk on his views and directions. The meeting came to a close at about 10:00 P.M. and everyone was thanked for attending.

 

Existing Agreements with Surrounding Municipalities: This discussion involved an update to the general membership to advise them that the work performed by Doug (VE3DGC) and Ed (VA3ED) and Bill (VE3TUC) and others in the Renfrew area regarding an agreement with Horton Township has been completed. The agreement was signed by the Renfrew County ARES EC as well as the Reeve and Clerk of Horton Township in July of 2004. Complete details of the agreement are available by request to the EC. I would point out that the agreement reads very much the same as the agreement with Renfrew County, however, a clarification on the topic of liability of ARES members was addressed prior to signing the agreement. It was during this process of clarification regarding liability to ARES members that the BY-LAW No. 2003-18 was introduced to ARES as a blanket coverage of liability during an emergency as specified in the By-Law that treats ARES members as employees of the Township, and therefore fall under the protection of the By-Law. Further discussion during the meeting resulted in the query whether or not we required a list of ARES members to be forwarded to the Clerk for record purposes. This is to be investigated, and acted upon if required.

 

OSARVA Update: Fred (VA3FPB) notified the group that he now is a certified instructor for OSARVA and has the power to re-certify our group as required. No further date for recertification has yet been determined.

SET 2004: Brodie (VA3BDT) addressed the group with the progress of SET 2004. He advised us that the SET this year was not a province wide SET, and that our intentions are to formally make a contact with neighboring ARES groups and to verify that a route for traffic can be determined. No official traffic will be passed. VHF and HF as well as any other mode available will be attempted. The date for SET this year is Saturday October 23/04, with things officially kicking off at 0900 local and running until 1500 local.

Upcoming ARES Events:

Goblin Patrol

A discussion on the Halloween Patrol, both nights (Saturday October 30'th and Sunday October 31,2004) will progress in the usual manner. Participants will advise Fred of their availability. Members that are participating in the Goblin Patrol this year will assemble in the parking lot of the Pembroke Police building at 1900 hours local both evenings. It is expected to be a busy patrol with the fact that the activities fall on a weekend. Fred made it known that at a recent attended meeting of the Lions club, Pembroke Police Chief Blair MacIsaac had alot of praise for the ARES group and in particular mentioned that the ARES Goblin patrol was a big help to the protection of people and property.

Cobden Santa Parade:

Jamie (VA3JRC) indicated that amateurs are once again asked to assist with the upcoming Santa Parade in Cobden Ontario. The date this year is November 27,2004 at 2:30 P.M. local. Jamie also presented the group with a thank you letter from the Cobden Santa Claus Parade Committee thanking members for their contribution in the past years. Members assist with crowd control and the overall safety and flow of the parade.

Guest Visitor and Speaker: Mike Hickey (VE3IPC) gave a prepared speech covering his background and his current status as the Ottawa District Emergency Coordinator. Mike included an overview of his duties as well as his intention in the future. Mike will be paying special regards to the intercommunications and capabilities of the ARES groups that fall under his watch. This includes Renfrew County, Ottawa, and Prescott Russell. A concerted effort will be made to keep the EC's of each respective area in touch with each other. Mike is also hoping to see a Packet network that can be utilized for communications between the groups, leading eventually to a province wide network. Mike is planning a meeting (date to be announced) sometime in February in Ottawa that will include all of the EC's and AEC's of the district. This will be an opportunity for each district to share with others their individual status and methods with their communities. Mike also indicated that he will work toward the goal of a continued and strong ARES presence in the district.
