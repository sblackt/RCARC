ARES AEC MEETING

Needs and Assessment (Con't)

April 28, 2004 - (1900 ~ 2100 hrs EST)

Meeting Location: Pembroke (Joe Street)

Attendance: VE3PL, VE3GQW, VA3FPB, VA3SUR, VA3AJT, VE3DGC

Regrets: VA3ED

 

This meeting with the Renfrew County ARES Assistant Emergency Coordinators (AEC's) was called to further develop the Needs and Assessment portion of the Renfrew County ARES group. The general thrust of this meeting was to define a course of action that could be taken to deal with the questions of the Needs and Assessment file, specifically the 'Needs of the Community' section. Our goal during this meeting was to find a way to either satisfy the questions, or lay-out a path that would lead us to find the answers to the questions.

The following is an outline of the discussions based on the questions posed during the previous general membership ARES meeting. For the purpose of this report, I will first indicate the question, followed by the discussion that ensued during the AEC meeting.

 

ITEM #1: Are we aware of the likelihood of the potential disasters in our communities?

Intro: My goal here is to highlight the possibility of an emergency facing our respective communities, or our County as a whole. What, if any, past studies have been completed and what is the highest risk scenario according to those studies?

                During the discussion regarding this question, it was agreed that those Municipalities that had completed their own Emergency Plan would have already discussed the potential for disasters. It was then obvious that access to this information was crucial in order for our ARES group to effectively assess  our abilities to respond. For those Municipalities that have not as of yet completed their Emergency Plan, where we currently have an assigned AEC or ARES representative sitting with the Emergency Planning Committee, our member will be responsible for reporting the findings of the emergency likelihood back to the ARES group to allow us to assess our position. For those Municipalities that have not as of yet prepared an Emergency Plan, or have not contacted our group, our position will be to be prepared for the eventual contact, and attempt to accommodate the requesting party at that time.

 

ITEM #2: Can we define what the disasters are likely to be?

Intro: If we are aware of the highest risks in our communities, then we can sway our resources into a given direction, while maintaining an open mind to the unknown. Can we label any of the high risk areas, or define where to find this information.

               As stated above, the Municipalities that have completed their Emergency Plan would have the information regarding their own local concerns. It would be our job to ensure that we are in the position to respond to those identified possibilities. This would include ensuring that communications would be possible, and in some cases, antennae installed in preparation for emergency communications from identified buildings that would be used as shelters and emergency operations.

 

ITEM #3: Are we aware of what our community leaders expect of us?

Intro: Let's resolve a manner in which all members can be clear on what our role is. Perhaps a defined mission statement or review of our Agreement with the County.

In the case of existing agreements, a review will be done to assess if there are areas that should be further clarified or modified. There remains the possibility that there may need to be additions to the agreement as well. In the case of the Municipalities that are currently working on drafting an agreement with ARES, the review of the County Agreement will serve to allow an updated agreement to be written and put in place. Bernie has agreed to review the existing agreement, and bring his findings forward for further discussions. It was also discussed that our general membership needs to be informed and trained in the areas that ARES has agreed to undertake.

 

ITEM #4: Can we be deployed to deal with massive loss of power and communications and successfully set up a network and/or count on our existing infrastructure to handle our defined tasks?

Intro: Where are our resources now? Can we coordinate those resources with other emergency services? Are the current resources adequate, or do we need to add more. How can we best maintain the current resources? (i.e.: assign someone to check on the condition of the resources and do light maintenance.) Can we put together an inventory of existing resources?

This lead to the discussion of the current infra-structure that ARES has in place. At this time, a list was prepared identifying the following locations that currently have antenna installations and in some cases additional radio equipment in an emergency box. The list included the following:

Pembroke Police Services Building
Pembroke Fire Department
Algonquin College, Pembroke Campus
Champlain Discovery School
Fellows High School
Bishop Smith Catholic High School
County of Renfrew Building
Laurention Public School
Red Cross Building - Pembroke
Renfrew Victoria Hospital
Point Alexander Municipal Building

A quick review of the installations indicated that most of this hardware had not been inspected for some time. It was brought to the table that an active ARES member (VA3JRC) indicated that he would be interested in checking the condition of these installations. Bob and Fred agreed to clarify the access location of the antenna and coax for the installations that they were familiar with. Bob suggested that Jamie would probably not need to travel to Point Alexander to check on that equipment, seeing as Bob resided in the area. I suspect that during follow-up we will initiate a plan that would see the existing equipment checked on a regular bases.

It was also noted that additional resources should be considered. A portable repeater would be a definite asset. The possibility of requesting assistance from the County regarding the purchasing of radio equipment was discussed. It should also be noted here that until we are aware of the areas of concern within the Municipalities we may not be totally aware of our requirements.

 

ITEM #5: Do we have a clear understanding of our roles when petitioned by a group within the community to assist with public functions?

Intro: Should we create some guidelines to follow? How do we go about preparing those guidelines? How do we best distribute those guidelines? Can we make the guidelines available to those requesting our assistance for community activities?

A quick discussion on this matter leaned toward the idea of dealing with the requests for our assistance from organizations  on an individual basis. A discussion of the request would be held by the AEC's and a decision made as to whether it would be in the best interest of ARES and the community for us to assist.

 Further discussion on this topic lead to the formation of several guidelines that the group could use to assist with the decision. They are listed below.

Does the request to assist with the function fall under the heading 'Community Services'?
Would the request allow our members to use our participation as a useful training exercise?
Would those members of our group participating be safe?
Do we have the required skills to perform the task requested of us?
Will our members be interested in participating?

 

 

ITEM #6: Do we understand the risks and potential liabilities imposed upon us by the community during a given function?

Intro: Are we prepared and qualified for the task at hand? Are we putting ourselves at risk? What are the governing factors to decide our safety? (i.e.: Pierce-Neige Car Rally). How can we minimize the risk?

Qualifications for the task at hand will vary greatly depending on the task. It will be important to understand the abilities of our members, and place them into positions that they are capable of handling. In order to meet with the requested tasks, it will be necessary to train our members in various areas. It is obvious that not all members would be interested in all areas of concern. Some would be more apt to be involved with Search and Rescue activities, and others would be more prone to be involved in traffic handling and related issues. This indicates the requirement for different exercises to allow for training in different areas. Some of the upcoming exercises were discussed, including Paddle Quest, SET2004, and Goblin Patrol.

Coordination of exercises were discussed, and the idea of a revolving coordinator was talked about, This would allow for various activities in various areas to be coordinated by different coordinators based on either a calendar schedule, or possibly be left to the requests made by community groups for our assistance.

The safety issue reference was brought forward, and as example, the car rally in particular was discussed as being a situation where even though our members had the skills to do the requested job, it was determined that the organizers of the activity failed to put in place specific safety measures to protect either our members or the general public. The example made reference to the situation where ARES members assisted with the communications at Riverside Park in Pembroke. The members where alerted via radio that a given race car would be approaching a given area on the track, and our members were expected to ensure that the track was clear and no members of the public would be placed in danger. This proved to be an impossible task, seeing as the organizers of the event refused to consider placing barricades along the route to keep the crowd back. They indicated that the placement of barriers would infer a liability on their part. We found that the general public would cross the path of the race cars freely in an attempt to get to the other side of the track. As EC, I felt that the whole situation was very poorly organized from a safety point, and withdrew the ARES resources from the activity for future events.

This pointed to the fact that aside from proper training, safety issues needed to be investigated on an individual event basis.

 

ITEM #7: Are we capable of assisting other communities with their emergencies, and if so what costs are we likely to incur?

Intro: Costs could include travel, possible lodging, food, and perhaps clothing. Are we prepared to leave our family behind? Are they secured prior to our departure?

The discussion on this question involved several issues. It was pointed out that contrary to some approaches used in emergencies involving working from the top down,  recently more weight has been put on the idea of working the response to assisting with an emergency from the bottom up. This implied that those that were responding to assist with an emergency have taken the time to ensure the safety of both their family and themselves. Readiness of our members is another concern. It was suggested that we use our training exercises to check that our ready bags were in fact complete and ready to be employed on short notice. Reminders should be passed on to the members, possibly via our weekly net, to check the status of our ready bags. Possibly a lecture could be arranged to inform our members the importance of these issues at a general members meeting.

 

ITEM #8: Is there a defined  memorandum of understanding (MOU) which we can refer to when called upon to assist with an emergency?

Intro: What areas if any in the current Agreement with the County needs revision? Should we have an MOU with each municipality that wishes to use ARES in time of emergency?

Because this question dealt very closely with Item #3, it was presumed dealt with during the dealings of Item #3.

 

ITEM #9: Can we communicate beyond the borders of our County effectively during an emergency?

Intro: Emergency communications beyond our County will take some coordination to be effective and immediately available when required. How can we best achieve this?

Correspondence between our neighboring ARES groups and ourselves would need to be carried out in order to effectively integrate our communication abilities with our neighbors. The vehicle in which to test our abilities would be the exercises that encompass communications beyond our County borders. This would include such activities as the annual Simulated Emergency Training (SET) exercise and our involvement with Field Day.

A follow up with our counterparts in Ottawa will be undertaken to bring them up to date with our current abilities with Packet. There was some discussion about Ottawa considering a gateway that would allow our Packet frequency to integrate with their frequency.

 

ITEM #10: What other concerns might we need to address when talking about public assistance?

Intro: List other areas of concern.

The idea that we should be presenting the general members with an update of upcoming events in order to maintain morale was discussed. It was thought that too much time spent on administrative issues would lead to a loss of interest by the members.

 

In closing, the tentative date of May 26'th 2004 was discussed for the next general membership meeting to be held for the Renfrew County ARES group.

As EC, I would like to thank the attending members for their input and effort in maintaining an effective ARES group in Renfrew County.
