Renfrew County ARES

'Needs and Assessment'

A general members meeting was held at the Pembroke Police Services building in Pembroke on March 31, 2004. This meeting is part of an ongoing build that the Renfrew County ARES group is currently working on. It followed an email blitz that took place in the previous month that saw every amateur in the County of Renfrew (where possible) receive an email inviting them to learn more about ARES. There were 21 members in attendance, several of which were new members.

Following introductions and a general business session, the group then began the Needs and Assessment portion of the meeting. The concept here is to raise awareness of the many questions that we may harbor as individuals but have not brought forward. It is the intention of this meeting to raise questions specific to our role as ARES members.

The segment was kicked off with this introductory speech presented by the EC.

"Joining the thrust of the ARES groups throughout Ontario, Renfrew County ARES and the amateurs of Renfrew County are in many ways leading the Province with the ability to respond to the needs of our community. This has been demonstrated in various fashions. Certainly one of the most obvious is the work done by the amateurs in conjunction with the Chalk River Region Nuclear Emergency Plan Exercise. This, along with the Search and Rescue abilities of our group is the envy of more than a few ARES groups province wide. Our agreement with the County of Renfrew has been the model several municipalities have and are using to fashion their own agreements with our organization. Recognition by our local police services as well as the OPP along with the aforementioned agreements and OSARVA training has resulted in Renfrew County ARES members becoming a vital link in the communities Emergency Plan."

 "I believe that in order to continue to meet the obligations we have set for ourselves, we need to look very hard and very seriously at our ARES group. I have often used the ice-storm as a reference when talking about our abilities. We, as amateurs opened the eyes of the community leaders, in fact, in most cases, we surprised them to the degree that we were given letters of recognition, awards of merit, and in a lot of cases, recognition from the Premier of the Province. But the truth is, we surprised them."

 "We as ARES members no longer have the element of surprise on our side. Our elected officials are now looking at us closely and have certain expectations of ARES. Moreover, the local municipalities that are counting on ARES, specifically within the Renfrew County, have certain expectations of ARES. This brings me to the next point. Are the members of Renfrew County Amateur Radio Emergency Services ready?"

 "I think the answer to the above is more complex than just a yes or no. I strongly believe that we are a lot more ready than we were during the ice storm. I also believe that if we were called upon tomorrow to provide our services for a municipal state of emergency, we would have a tough time meeting the expectations of our community. This is the matter at hand, the one I wish to address at this meeting. Renfrew County amateur radio operators are some of the finest of the finest. Renfrew County is an extremely large county, with numerous municipalities, and numerous elected officials, and numerous potential hazards. This makes for a challenge for any EC. If there is any light at the end of the tunnel, it is in the resources that our local amateur radio operators offer. All of the resources in the world however, does not solve all of the problems all of the time. The best we can hope for is to meet realistic expectations from those who count on us. In order to do this task, those resources require good management. When I review our obligation to the community, it becomes apparent that mere existence is no longer an acceptable answer. Add to that the recent growth of interest in ARES from our friends in Arnprior, Renfrew, Horton and Greater Madawaska, and it becomes real clear that effective management is no longer just one meeting a year, but rather a complete re-assessment of our local group."

 "Many of you here tonight are aware that there has been a significant ‘behind the scenes’ effort to kick start the year for ARES by initiating contact with all amateurs in the County where possible, and inviting them to learn more about ARES. Many of you here tonight have been involved with ARES for a number of years, and some of you are here for the first time. In our continued effort to build our group to a state of readiness, I hope with your help to complete a needs and assessment of the Renfrew County ARES group."

 "To this end I have prepared a list of questions that are open for discussion amongst one another. The results of which when compiled will reflect many of our strengths and our weaknesses.  I do not expect nor do I anticipate to be able to solve many of the issues at hand tonight, however, if we can establish the areas that clearly need attention, than this meeting will have served its purpose. A follow up meeting is scheduled for the end of May, at which time I would hope to see some resolve to the issues we identify tonight."

 "Let’s get started."

Questions Posed to and By the Membership

Understanding the needs of the community:

(1) Are we aware of the likelihood of the potential disasters in our communities?

(2) Can we define what disasters there are likely to be?

(3) Are we aware of what our community leaders expect of us?

(4) Can we be deployed to deal with massive loss of power and communications and successfully set up a network and/or count on our existing infrastructure to handle our defined tasks?

(5) Do we have a clear understanding of our roles when petitioned by a group within the community to assist with public functions?

(6) Do we understand the risks and potential liabilities imposed upon us by the community during a given function?

(7) Are we capable of assisting other communities with their emergencies, and if so what costs are we likely to incur?

(8) Is there a defined Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) which we can refer to when called upon to assist with an emergency?

(9)  Can we communicate beyond the borders of our County effectively during an emergency?

(10) What other concerns might we need to address when talking about public assistance?

(M) Does 9/11 affect our roles, and if so how? (i.e.: modes of communication, biological, etc.,)

Understanding the needs of the membership:

(1)   Do we have a clear understanding of our role as ARES members?

(2)   Are we aware of what the community expects of us?

(3)   Is there a full understanding of our obligations to the community?

(4)   Can we currently expect to fulfill our obligations?

(5)   How can we better prepare for an emergency situation that requires us to handle large volumes of traffic?

(6)   Do all of our members have adequate training, and if not, how can we rectify this?

(7)   Can we effectively communicate with our neighboring ARES groups, and what steps can we take to ensure reliable communications around the province?

(8)   Are we as members assisting the community protected from liabilities and to what extent if any are we insured?

(9)    Do we have a mechanism in place that will allow for the flow of internal traffic amongst members of our group whether in a state of emergency or not?

(10)    What can we do as a group to address these and other issues the membership may have?

(M) Is there a priority list as in who gets attention first and if so who sets the priorities?

(M) Do we require or should we have an operating handbook that spells out the standard operating procedure (SOP) in a given situation?

(M) Should we be looking at the way we handle traffic, when dealing with large volumes, should we be categorizing it and using more than one frequency to handle the various type of traffic?

(M) Have we addressed the issue of personal readiness, are we prepared to leave our house and family to deal with others, have we looked after #1 first?

(M) Do we require a basic operations handbook?

(M) Where can we get assistance with funding for some of the equipment requirements to meet with the requirements of fulfilling our respective roles?

 Following the debate regarding questions, several avenues were discussed toward resolving these issues. Members were left with the questions to decide on an approach (i.e. groups, ways and means committee, individuals etc.,) to work on ways to find answers.

Several closing statements were made by the EC and by the members. The meeting was then adjourned.
