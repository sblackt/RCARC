MINUTES

AMATEUR RADIO EMERGENCY SERVICE

Planning Meeting

ARNPRIOR 18 JANUARY 2004

 

 

Opening Comments

 

Peter VE3YYY called the meeting to order at 13:00 hours.  He welcomed the delegates and acknowledged the presence of  Deputy Mayor Nancy Black representing the Town of Arnprior,  Malcom Montgomery, Executive Assistant to MP Cheryl Gallant and Doug Leach, Regional Director for Radio Amateurs of Canada.

 

Welcome

 

Nancy Black welcomed the group to Arnprior on behalf of Mayor Gibeau and Council.  She spoke briefly about emergency planning in Arnprior and the degree to which Amateur Radio has been integrated into their planning process.

 

Malcom Montgomery, representing Cheryl Gallant MP, welcomed the group.

He spoke of the changes in Emergency Measures Canada as a direct result of 9/11 and the increased awareness regarding the need for home-land security at the Federal level.  The organization has been moved from within National Defense to a stand-alone Department with a Minister directly responsible for the development of plans, policy and funding.  Malcom made the point that although Municipalities will be given increased responsibility for developing local emergency plans, it is uncertain at this time the degree of funding from the Federal Government which would be made available.

Malcom pointed out that the presence of the AECL reactor at Chalk River required a more detailed emergency plan in municipalities downstream of the reactor and the need for more tightly integrated communications links between them.

 

Introductions

 

All present introduced themselves and the organization they were representing.

 

ARES Presentation

 

Les Thom VE3PL, ARES Emergency Coordinator for Renfrew County, described the organizational structure of the Amateur Radio Emergency Service.  He outlined briefly the role of the Service, the responsibility of each division and how ARES interfaces with the Radio Amateurs of Canada.

Les described the Agreement which had been signed between ARES and Renfrew County  covering the role and responsibilities of ARES during emergencies and between the OPP and ARES outlining the role of ARES in Search and Rescue activities.

He also stressed the need for Amateurs to be involved in community emergency exercises.   Les recommended that Municipal Emergency Coordinators meet periodically with local Amateur Radio Emergency Coordinators (ARES)  in order to discuss changes and to develop a good working relationship.  They should not meet for the first time during an emergency!

 

Les stressed the point that Amateur radio facilities can only be used by licensed Amateurs and can receive no monetary remuneration beyond ‘out-of-pocket-expenses’.  Planners should view this service as a “Back-up to a back-up” when purchasing or installing commercial radio equipment for use in their Municipalities.

 

Amateur Facilities in Renfrew County

 

Representatives of local radio clubs provided details of the four repeaters in Renfrew County, which are available to support emergency activities.  In every instance the Amateur Club’s Constitutions commit the clubs to make the repeaters available to ARES for emergency activities.

 

Coffee Break

 

During coffee break attendees were given a tour of the VE3YYX repeater and were able to see several amateur radios, which were on display.

Peter demonstrated a digital mode of communications (IRLP) by connecting a hand held radio to McMurdo Sound in Antarctica and by linking repeater VE3YYX to VE3NRR.

 

APRS

 

Dave VE3KIM gave a brief presentation on the Automatic Position Reporting System (APRS).  The system allows the position of APRS equipped vehicles to be displayed in real time on a computer map of the area and also provides a text messaging system.  Dave suggested that amateurs with APRS installed in their vehicles could provide Emergency Coordinators with a visual display of their deployed assets.

 

Government Funding

 

Chief Burnette spoke of Arnprior’s success in obtaining funding through the

Joint Emergency Preparedness Program (JEPP) to purchase a radio repeater for use by the Amateur community in support of local emergencies. 

In response to a question, Chief Burnette agreed to make a copy of the Arnprior application for a JEPP grant available to other municipalities for reference.

 

Amateur Licensing

 

Peter spoke briefly about the licensing process for Amateurs.  He advised that Les Thom and himself were qualified instructors as well as Delegated Examiners on behalf of the Federal Government and are committed to provide a course to any volunteers or employees of local communities who wish to acquire the Basic Amateur Radio license.

 

Open discussion

 

A lengthy free flowing exchange of information and personal views developed on many topics.  This is a very brief summary of the discussions.

 

Chief Naismith spoke of the effort that Greater Madawaska was making in developing an Emergency Plan and the need for assistance in identifying the best communications network to cover a very large area.  He also would appreciate a copy of the Arnprior application to the JEPP program.

 

Representatives from Greater Madawaska posed several questions.  Replies were as follows:

 

            Amateurs will assist in designing and setting up emergency communication

            links.  Wayne, VE3JSQ should be used as liaison with ARES;

 

Amateurs will respond to a request for support following the declaration of an

Emergency by the Township.  ARES is the means through which support is

requested as some amateurs may be brought in from other areas.  Les Thom is the

contact for Municipalities with-in Renfrew County at (613) 582-3547.

 

The Municipality should consider pre-positioning facilities (towers, antennas etc.)

at remote areas of the township, for use by Amateurs during emergencies.

 

The Municipality should consider entering into a formal agreement with ARES

for emergency support.

 

The representative from McNab/Braeside requested a copy of the Renfrew County

agreement between ARES and the County.  He also needs support in determining radio coverage and the names of Amateurs in the area.  Les Thom agreed to assist in these areas.

 

A lengthy discussion ensued around the issues of liability, insurance coverage, coverage under the Workman’s Compensation regulations etc.  Given the developing

Government (Federal/Provincial) positions on the integration of Amateurs into Municipal Emergency Plans the consensus was that these issues should be clarified by ARES/RAC with Industry Canada on behalf of Canadian Amateurs.

 

Doug Leach made the point that Municipalities should enter into formal agreements with ARES.  This approach simplifies response planning, the integration of Amateurs into the Municipal plan and may help to address the issues of insurance coverage, liability etc.

 

Peter VE3BQP made the point that the majority of ARES activities in emergencies were in support of health and welfare issues through the Red Cross and/or the Salvation Army.  He stressed that planning by ARES and local municipalities should also include discussions with these organizations.

 

Rick VE3IHI advised that ARES in the U.S.A. have standardized on a common wire connector.  This will ensure interoperability of equipment owned by Amateurs brought in from other areas during emergencies.  He is attempting through ARES to have Canadian amateurs adopt this hardware also.  Anderson Power Pole manufactures the connectors.

 

 

Closing Comments

 

Peter VE3YYY thanked everyone for attending and participating.

 

He emphasized the willingness of the Amateur Community through ARES, to support municipalities during emergencies and suggested that they contact local Amateurs for assistance in setting up backup communications networks.

 

The meeting closed at 16:00 hours.
